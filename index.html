Mask Optimization for Image Inpainting Using No-Reference Image Quality Assessment

Taiki Uchiyama, Mariko Isogawa
Keio University
IEEE Open Journal of Signal Processing, Vol. 6, 2025
Paper Code

Mask Optimization: The optimization of the masked region improves the quality of inpainting. Our method achieves pixel-level optimization reflecting human visual perception.

Abstract

Image inpainting is a technique designed to remove unwanted regions from images and restore them. This technique is expected to be applied in various applications, including image editing, virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), and augmented reality (AR). Typically, the inpainting process is based on missing regions predefined by user-applied masks. However, the specified areas may not always be ideal for inpainting, and the quality of the inpainting results varies depending on the annotated masked region. Therefore, this paper addresses the task of generating masks that improve inpainting results. To this end, we proposed a method that utilized No-Reference Image Quality Assessment (NR-IQA), which can score image quality without a reference image, to generate masked regions that maximize inpainting quality.

Proposed Framework

Our framework automatically generates an optimal masked region $M_{pred}$ to maximize the perceptual quality score of the inpainting results given an input image $I_{input}$ and an initial user mask $M_{ini}$.

  • Inpainting Module & STE: To enable end-to-end gradient-based optimization through binary masks, we employ the Straight-Through Estimator (STE) to handle non-differentiable thresholding functions.
  • Mask Dilation Module: An adaptive, patch-based iterative loop mechanism that selectively dilates mask boundaries based on localized quality scores from the lightweight NIMA model, preventing the optimization from getting stuck due to insufficient context coverage.

Loss Functions

The entire architecture is trained end-to-end using a joint loss function formulated as:

$$\mathcal{L} = \alpha\mathcal{L}_{I} + \beta\mathcal{L}_{D} + \gamma\mathcal{L}_{B}$$

Where $\mathcal{L}_{I}$ represents the Inpainting Score Loss based on Q-Align quality score differences, $\mathcal{L}_{D}$ is the Dice Loss, and $\mathcal{L}_{B}$ is the Boundary Loss to maintain mask shape consistency and prevent erratic speckled pattern expansions.

Quantitative Results

Our method demonstrates superior performance against baseline configurations (the raw initial mask $M_{ini}$ and static uniform dilation) across multiple state-of-the-art NR-IQA evaluation metrics (Q-Align, TOPIQ, LIQE, MANIQA) on both the COCO Animal and Object datasets.

User Study

A perceptual pairwise user study (11 participants, 20 test cases) rated image naturalness from 1 to 10. Our optimized masks achieved an average naturalness score of 6.7, significantly outperforming the initial unoptimized masks at 4.8.

Limitations

Optimization challenges remain when dealing with heavily blurred objects or ambiguous boundaries where the local texture signals fail. Furthermore, the framework currently handles spatially disconnected object components caused by severe occlusions poorly.
